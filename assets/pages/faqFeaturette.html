What is Paper online tutoring?

Paper partners with school districts to deliver 1:1 tutoring that is equitable, scalable, and cost-effective. Students get unlimited 24/7 live help and writing feedback, teachers get insights to tackle individual learning gaps, and administrators get actionable data to inform strategic decisions. Paper’s multilingual tutors add an extra layer of support across all content areas and grade levels, in and out of the classroom— so students always have access to expert help exactly when, where, and how they need it.

Who are Paper’s tutors?

Paper carefully selects every member of our diverse and multilingual team of tutors to ensure students get the very best help. We hire high-performing college students, PhD candidates, and teachers with expertise across K-12 subject areas.

Are Paper tutors free?

Paper believes every student should have access to the support they need—anytime, anywhere. We also believe that the burden of providing this support shouldn’t be on teachers or parents. Paper partners with school systems to deliver 24/7, unlimited, and multilingual tutoring—at no cost to families.

How do I check if I have access to Paper?

Check out our access lookup tool to see if you have access to Paper through your school district or state.

Is Paper tutoring legit?

Yes! Paper is currently partnered with hundreds of school systems across North America, serving nearly 2.5 million students on our 24/7 academic support platform. Learn more about Paper's impact here.

How does Paper tutoring work?

Paper offers free, 24/7 chat-based tutoring for students in English, Spanish, French, and Mandarin. Tutors are available on-demand for unlimited, 1:1 support in any K-12 subject area. Based on a student’s question, grade level, and language preference, Paper pairs students with the best tutor to meet their needs within 30 seconds. Transcripts of each tutoring session are saved for students whenever they need to revise concepts.

Along with live help, Paper also offers an asynchronous essay review tool that allows students to receive annotated feedback on content, grammar, and structure.
